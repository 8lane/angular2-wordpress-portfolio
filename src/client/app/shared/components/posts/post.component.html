Introduction

In January 2016, following a successful re-brand project, I was tasked with the design & development of an ambitious HTML5 webapp residing within the Adobe Premiere Pro ecosystem. In a scrum team including myself & two back-end engineers, we delivered the project in two week cycles over a period of several months

Key contributions

UX/UI design providing initial sketches to high fidelity AI mockups

Contributing towards user research, paper prototypes, wireframes & A/B tests

Used Node to setup front-end build tools for SASS compilation, Autoprefixer & TypeScript / CSS linting

Developed the UI and worked on core functionality written in TypeScript

Modular, decoupled, Angular2 component based SASS written using BEM syntax

Provided services to communicate with backend API’s and Adobe's CEP bridge

User stories

With previous experience largely consisting of waterfall based projects, working for the first time in an Agile environment proved extremely rewarding. Daily stand ups, weekly story grooming, two week sprints along with sprint reviews & retrospectives worked well for me personally as well as the team. Whilst the team adopted a fairly rigid Agile/Scrum approach — I always felt an urgency to improve or iterate the process.

User stories, chores and tech debt was created during the project by everyone within the team. This allowed us to all contribute towards defining acceptance criteria that needed to be met in order for the story to be complete. Once the criteria were met, the story would be verified by a product owner and then tested by QA tester.

Development

As a design minded developer, building this app along with two back-end engineers [Mike Tomaras & Shukri Tacho] turned out to be great combination for the project in general but also my own development. Exposure to TDD and strongly typed programming was a massive step in my learning

Angular 2

Modular, component based uni-directional framework that encouraged clean, modern and fast performing Javascript

ECMAScript 6

Using Babel as a transpiler, key features such as classes, module imports, arrow functions & template strings were put to full use

Jasmine & Karma

In a TDD style, unit tests were written for all components; run locally as well as in our continuous integration build

Bootstrap framework

A tailored version of Bootstrap (Variables, Grid, Navigation, Dropdown & Buttons) served as a light foundation that could be easily expanded

BEM syntax

The naming convention was strictly adopted throughout providing clarity & scalability for potential future developers

Powerful mixins & functions

Repetitive or mundane tasks such as dynamic media queries or brand color variations were solved by creating quick & easy mixins

Graceful animations

Micro interactions in the form of subtle animation were heavily adopted but only for providing context to a particular feature

Outcome

After finishing beta testing in early September 2016, the app was officially launched at Adobe’s IBC conference in Amsterdam. Along with Audio Network’s own marketing, Adobe’s Twitter shout out, and Youtube reviews here & here, we immediately saw large influx of users engaging and generating revenue within the app

Ecommerce experience

Auditioning music within Premiere Pro for free is a video editor’s dream. Selecting & paying for a license is not which is why this necessary task should be quick and painless. Angular 2’s two-way data binding make creating intuitive forms a breeze. The form validation should also serve as an aid, not a distraction. Payment is handled real-time, in app using Braintree's API

Optimised for all configurations

One of several common occurances from our facilitated usability tests were how users docked or resized the plugin window in a variety of ways. This evidence was enough for me to decide on a ‘mobile first’ approach to building the app with an absolute minimum size of 320px
